The Righteous Mind

The Righteous Mind: Why Good People are Divided by Politics and Religion by Jonathan Haidt is a terrific read and was recommended to me by my younger brother. In Part 1 of this book, Intutions Come First, Strategic Reasonin Come Second, Haidt outlines three possible theories for how passion and reasoning should interact within our minds. The three theories are from PLato, Thomas Jefferson, and David Hume. Each are explained and then experiments are done to prove which one is correct.

Each of the theories involve two charaters: Reason and Passion. Reason is the logic behind decisions and the vocalalzition of why we do something. Reason is represented by a person. Passion is our intutions and moral decisions. Passion is our gut instict and is represented by an elephant.

Plato: Reason is King

Jefferson: Co-Leaders

Hume: Reason is Slave
