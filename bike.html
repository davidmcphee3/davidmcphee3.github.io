Bike Maintenance

This is a bike maintenance schedule that I have made for myself and am hosting on the website simply so I do not lose track of the list.

Preface

This schedule is for commutting year-round in Saskatoon, through bike paths and some roads.

Daily Care - End of Day

  1. Clean Drivetrain
    • Remove any foreign moisture and objects from all components
    • Use rag between sprockets to get out any grime
  2. Check Tires
    • Squeeze test on tires
    • Spin tires and check for trueness
    • Look over tread for any debris and damage
  3. Large Debris Removal
    • Look over entirety of bike and remove large pieces of mud, snow, branches, etc
    • Warm water and soft brush on visible dirt
    • Wipe dry

Weekly Care - Sunday Afternoons

  1. Degrease
    • Spray each component with degrease
    • Use toothbrush to scrub derailleur pulleys
    • Use chain degreaser box on chain
    • Rinse and wipe off excess degreaser
  2. Lubricate
    • Place 1 drop of lube on each chain link - extra lube is a bad thing
    • Wipe off excess lube if need be
    • Place 1 drop on each hinge, pulley brushing, seal, and derailleur spring
    • Place 1 drop on pedal springs if need be
  3. Bolt Check
    • Tighten every bolt from top to bottom
  4. Drop Check
    • Drop bike (don’t let it fall over) from about 8 inches and listen for abnormalities
  5. Test Brakes
    • If brakes feel soft, check brake pads
    • If pads are less than a dimes width, replace
  6. Check Tire Pressure
    • Use pump and gauge to maintain tire pressure
  7. Wiggle and Smoothness Test
    • Wiggle tests for any unwanted play in each component
    • Smoothness test for any hesitations or stiffness in each component
    • Pedal cranks
    • Brake arms
    • Brake levers
    • Derailleurs
    • Suspension forks
    • Shocks
    • Headset
    • Any other moving bike part
  8. Drivetrain Check
    • Check shifting between all gears
      • Chain should not hesitate or get held up
    • If bike is not shifting great:
      • Tighten the cable at the retainer bolt end
      • Make fine adjustments with the barrel adjuster
      • If the chain comes off or gets caught whilst shifting:
        • Adjust the adjustment screws
        • “H” is high gear limit adjuster (smaller sprocket)
        • “L” is low gear limit adjuster (larger sprocket)

Start of Summer Care - 1st Sunday of May

  1. Chain Elongation
    • The distance between one rivet and 23 rivets downstream should be 12’ +- 1/16”
      • If the chain fails this test, replace it
    • A damaged chain will lead to malformation of the teeth in the cassette and chainring
  2. Derailleur Hanger
    • Wiggle, crank test
  3. Cable Check
    • If braking/shifting is sluggish, cable may need to be replaced

Middle of Summer Care - 1st Sunday of Aug

  1. Chain Elongation
    • The distance between one rivet and 23 rivets downstream should be 12’ +- 1/16”
      • If the chain fails this test, replace it
    • A damaged chain will lead to malformation of the teeth in the cassette and chainring
  2. Possible Chain Replacement
    • If the chain has not been replaced in “Start of Summer Care”, replace now
  3. Rear Wheel Cassette
    • Check the teeth, should be symmetrical and not hooked or sharp
    • If the chain is replaced regularly, the cassette will last longer
    • Use wiggle test on cassette
  4. Bearings
    • All rotating components have a bearing mechanism
    • Wheels, headset, pedals, bottom brackets
    • All parts should be taken apart and re-greased
  5. Shock Rebuild
    • Dismantle, inspect, and clean
    • Seals and rings may need to be replaced
    • Inner workings should be oiled and greased
  6. Replace failing parts
    • Handlebar, stem, suspension fork, seatpost, saddle
      • Check for rust on metal parts, may never need to be replaced
      • Carbon fiber parts should be replaced every 3 - 5 years
      • Check saddle for cushion wear
